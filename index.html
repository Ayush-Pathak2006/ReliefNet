Be Prepared for Emergencies

Disasters can strike with little warning. Having an emergency plan can save lives.

  • Water (one gallon per person per day for several days)
  • Non-perishable food (at least a three-day supply)
  • Battery-powered radio and extra batteries
  • Flashlight and extra batteries
  • First aid kit and medications
  • Emergency contact information

Create a family emergency plan that includes:

  • Meeting locations (both in your neighborhood and outside your area)
  • Emergency contact information
  • Evacuation routes from your home and neighborhood
  • How to shut off utilities
  • Special needs considerations for family members

  • Stay calm and follow your emergency plan
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for instructions
  • Evacuate if authorities recommend it
  • Check on neighbors, especially elderly or disabled
  • Use text messaging instead of phone calls to communicate
  • Press the SOS button on ReliefNet if you need immediate assistance
